Brilliant Color — BC-6 Chemical Stocking

Brilliant Color is a small supplier of chemicals and equipment used by photographic stores to process 35mm film. John Kubick normally stocks 11, 12, or 13 cases of BC-6 each week. Each case sold yields a profit of $35. If not sold, each case is discarded at a loss of $56. Probabilities: P(sell 11) = 0.45, P(sell 12) = 0.35, P(sell 13) = 0.20.
a) Decision Table — Payoff Calculations

c) Impact of Stabilizing Ingredient
If BC-6 no longer has to be discarded, the $56 loss penalty for overstocking is removed. Unsold cases simply remain in inventory for the following week.
EMV (Stock 11) = $385 (same as before — demand always at least 11)

22.21
Conclusion

Conclusion about Probabilities and EVPI (from 22.19 and 22.20)

Balanced (0.5, 0.5)
EVPI = 20
Probabilities equal
More certain (0.75, 0.25)
EVPI = 10
One state more likely
Almost certain (0.95, 0.05)
EVPI = 2
Nearly certain outcome
When probabilities are balanced (0.5, 0.5)→ EVPI = 20
When one state becomes more certain (0.75, 0.25)→ EVPI = 10
When one state is almost certain (0.95, 0.05)→ EVPI = 2
📌
Conclusion: As probabilities become more certain, EVPI decreases. The value of perfect information diminishes when the outcome is already highly predictable.
